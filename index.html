📖 Laptop Buying Guide — What to Check

⚙️ Processor

For under ₹50,000, look for AMD Ryzen 5 (5000/7000 series) or Intel Core i5 (11th Gen or higher). Avoid Celeron/Pentium at this price — they'll feel sluggish quickly and age poorly.

🧠 RAM

8GB is acceptable for basic use, but 16GB is ideal for heavy multitaskers. Prefer DDR5 or LPDDR5 where possible. Always check if the laptop allows RAM upgrades post-purchase.

💾 Storage (SSD)

Always insist on an NVMe SSD — never settle for HDD at this price. A 512GB SSD is the sweet spot. Check for an extra M.2 slot if you may need more storage later.

🖥️ Display

Prefer IPS panels over TN for better viewing angles and colour accuracy. Full HD (1920×1080) is essential. Anti-glare coating is a bonus for bright room or outdoor use.

🔋 Battery Life

Target laptops with 40WHr+ battery capacity. Real-world life is always less than the claimed spec — aim for 7+ hours of genuine all-day comfort without hunting for a charger.

🔌 Ports & Connectivity

Ensure USB-A, USB-C, HDMI, and 3.5mm audio jack are all present. Wi-Fi 6 support is now a modern essential. Bluetooth 5.0+ enables smooth wireless peripherals without lag.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better under ₹50,000 — AMD or Intel? ▾
Both work well at this price. AMD Ryzen chips generally offer better battery efficiency and stronger integrated GPU performance. Intel's 12th Gen brings competitive burst performance. For most users in 2026, AMD Ryzen 5 (7000 series) is the smarter pick at this budget.
Is 8GB RAM enough in 2026? ▾
For light users — yes. For those running Chrome with 20+ tabs, coding IDEs, or design tools — 16GB is strongly preferred. Many laptops allow post-purchase RAM upgrades, so check compatibility before buying.
Should I buy online or from a local store? ▾
Amazon.in is the safest option — genuine products, easy returns, and competitive pricing. Avoid grey-market deals that void manufacturer warranties. Always buy from "Sold by Amazon" or verified authorised sellers.
Can these laptops handle gaming? ▾
Light titles like Minecraft, older GTA titles (low settings), or browser games are fine. For serious gaming, look at dedicated GPU laptops in the ₹60,000–₹80,000 range. These laptops are optimised for productivity, not gaming workloads.
